\# Executive Memo



\## Exposure and Analytical Framework



Our firm expects to receive a euro-denominated payment equivalent to approximately $12.5 million in one year. Given the current spot rate of 1.15 USD/EUR, this receivable is subject to foreign exchange risk, particularly if the euro depreciates against the U.S. dollar prior to settlement. To evaluate and manage this risk effectively, ratio analysis provides a structured framework for assessing financial exposure, hedge effectiveness, and overall impact on firm performance.



\## Importance of Ratio Analysis



Ratio analysis is critical in this context because it translates complex financial data into interpretable metrics that support decision-making. It allows us to quantify how different hedging strategies affect cash flows, risk exposure, and financial stability. By applying ratio-based evaluation, we can compare outcomes across multiple hedging approaches under consistent assumptions.



\## Key Ratio Categories



Six core ratio categories guide this analysis. \*\*Performance ratios\*\* assess overall returns generated from hedging strategies. \*\*Profitability ratios\*\* evaluate how hedging impacts net proceeds from the receivable. \*\*Efficiency ratios\*\* measure how effectively financial resources are utilized in executing hedge strategies. \*\*Leverage ratios\*\* examine the degree to which borrowing is used, particularly relevant for money market hedges. \*\*Liquidity ratios\*\* assess the firm’s ability to meet short-term obligations while maintaining hedge positions. Finally, the \*\*DuPont framework\*\* integrates profitability, efficiency, and leverage into a comprehensive view of financial performance, allowing us to understand how each hedge affects return on equity.



\## Analytical Plan and Next Steps



The next phase of analysis will incorporate current market data, including the one-year forward rate of 1.0910, U.S. risk-free rate of 3.713%, and euro risk-free rate of 2.722%, to construct a comparative hedging model. This model will evaluate forward contracts, money market hedges, and option-based strategies across a range of exchange rate scenarios. Sensitivity analysis will be applied to assess the impact of currency fluctuations on projected USD proceeds.



This structured approach will enable us to identify the optimal hedging strategy based on risk reduction, cost efficiency, and alignment with the firm’s financial objectives.
